The situation

In Florida, the first 14 days decide the coverage.

After a car accident, a patient's Personal Injury Protection benefits are capped at $2,500. That cap only lifts when a qualified provider documents an Emergency Medical Condition. With the determination on file, the available benefit opens to the full $10,000.

The complication is time. Care must begin within fourteen days of the accident, and a properly documented evaluation is not always easy to get quickly. When it slips, the patient loses coverage and the case loses value.

Questions, answered

What is an EMC evaluation, and why does it matter?+
An Emergency Medical Condition determination is a documented finding by a qualified provider. Under Florida PIP law it is what unlocks the full $10,000 in benefits instead of the $2,500 default cap.
How quickly can a patient be seen?+
Most patients are seen the same day they reach out, seven days a week. Because everything is telehealth, there is no travel and no waiting room for an injured patient.
Is the 14-day deadline strict?+
Florida PIP requires that initial care begin within 14 days of the accident. Same-day telehealth is the most reliable way to get a compliant determination documented inside that window.
How do attorneys refer patients?+
Through the secure attorney referral portal. Submit a client in about two minutes, track the status, and receive the signed report directly.
How are reports delivered?+
Signed determinations are delivered securely, typically within 48 hours of the evaluation, formatted and ready for the patient file or demand package.
Is my information kept private?+
Yes. Intake, consent, and video visits run on HIPAA-conscious, encrypted systems, with strict safeguards for protected health information.
